Mass-Energy Equivalence as a Coherence-Licensed Rest-Frame Relation
The equation usually written E = mc² is often introduced as a conversion between mass and energy.
That is not wrong, but it is easy to hear the phrase too loosely. The rest-frame form of the equation says something more precise:
E₀ = mc²
A bounded system does not carry one burden as inertia and a separate burden as rest-energy.
In its rest frame, the bounded whole carries one invariant burden, read in two registers.
The inertial register says what burden must be answered to accelerate the whole.
The energy register says what rest-frame energy burden belongs to the whole.
The equation holds when those are not two accounts that merely happen to agree. They are two registers of one whole-owned invariant.
In this artifact, c² will not be treated as a loose conversion slogan, nor as a decorative constant attached to a famous equation. It is the metric-determined translator between the inertial and energy registers. It expresses how the Lorentz-invariant structure of the spacetime comparison surface relates the two registers of the same bounded rest-frame whole.
The claim here is not that Structural Flow derives all of relativity. The claim is narrower:
Structural Flow identifies the structural object classically expressed by the rest-frame equation E₀ = mc²: a Coherence-closed bounded rest-frame whole carrying one whole-owned invariant, of which inertial answerability and total bound rest-energy are two registers. 
Scope
This artifact concerns the rest-frame seam of mass-energy equivalence within this universe as presented. 
It does not attempt to derive the full relativistic energy-momentum relation:
E² = p²c² + m²c⁴
That broader relation is the parent classical home of the rest-frame equation. The present burden is the p = 0 seam, where the whole-system momentum has been removed from the question and the system is considered in its own rest frame.
At that seam, the relation becomes:
E₀ = mc²
This artifact also does not derive the measured value of c, the full stress-energy tensor, general relativity, particle masses, the Higgs mechanism, quantum field theoretic mass generation, or every concrete binding-energy mechanism.
Those are not defects in the present argument. They are boundaries.
The present question is structural:
What object must be in place for the rest-frame equation to be lawful rather than arbitrary?
Or, more sharply:
What fails if E₀ = mc² fails as a relation?
The Failure Signature
The failure of E₀ = mc² is not that mass disappears.
It is not that energy disappears.
It is not that conservation fails.
It is not that a process such as fission, fusion, decay, or annihilation fails to occur.
It is not that c fails as the invariant propagation ceiling.
The structural failure of E₀ = mc² occurs when a bounded rest-frame whole retains intelligible inertial answerability and intelligible total bound rest-energy accounting, but those two registers no longer close as one invariant burden.
In shorter form: 
The bounded whole still has inertia. It still has rest-energy. But the two accounts no longer agree as one invariant burden.
This is the distinctive failure of the rest-frame mass-energy relation. It is a relation failure, not a side failure.
On the inertia side, the bounded system may still resist acceleration.
On the energy side, the bounded system may still carry energetic contributions.
But if those two registers no longer answer as one whole-owned invariant, the rest-frame equation has lost its structural object.
In Structural Flow terms, the primary failure regime is Drift.
The registers remain intelligible, but their alignment as one invariant burden drifts apart. The bounded whole now carries one account for motion-change and another account for rest-energy. It remains describable, but it is no longer one Coherence-held rest-frame burden.
That is what E₀ = mc² prevents.
The Bounded Rest-Frame Whole
The rest-frame equation does not begin with a free-floating quantity.
It begins with a bounded whole.
A bounded whole is not merely a spatial lump. It is a system whose contributions can be accounted as belonging to one object rather than merely occurring nearby.
The boundary need not be a hard wall. It may be physical, relational, dynamical, or accounting-based. What matters is that an inside/outside distinction is strong enough for whole-object accounting.
A composite particle may be bounded.
A nucleus may be bounded.
A gas in a container may be bounded.
A field contribution inside a control volume may be bounded for accounting purposes.
A stressed, heated, compressed, or internally moving system may be bounded if those internal contributions belong to the same whole.
The question is not simply where the object is.
The question is what belongs to the same rest-frame whole. 
If the contributions cannot be owned by one bounded whole, then the rest-frame equation has no object to govern.
If they can be owned by one bounded whole, then the system can present two registers: an inertial register and an energy register.
The Inertial Register
The inertial register asks what burden must be answered to change the motion-state of the bounded whole.
This is not merely persistence.
A thing may persist without its resistance to whole-system acceleration being held as one invariant burden.
This is not merely boundary.
Boundary says what belongs to the system. Inertia says what must be answered when the bounded system is accelerated as a whole.
This is not momentum.
Momentum may vanish in the rest frame. Inertial rest-load does not vanish merely because the whole is at rest.
This is not the equivalence of inertial and gravitational mass.
Gravitational mass, source-profile, and source/geometry coupling belong to neighboring questions. The rest-frame equation considered here concerns the inertial burden of the bounded whole.
Structurally, inertial rest-load is the whole-owned burden that must be answered to accelerate the bounded system as a whole. 
If inertial rest-load fails, the system may still remain identifiable. It may still have a boundary. It may still contain energetic contributions. But there is no same load that motion-change must answer to.
The thing remains, but no same burden governs motion-change.
That is the failure of the inertial register.
The Energy Register
The energy register asks what energetic burden belongs to the bounded whole when whole-system motion is removed from the question.
This is not generic energy.
Energy may appear as motion, heat, transfer, radiation, field excitation, stress, binding, or work capacity.
The rest-frame energy register asks a narrower question:
Which energetic contributions belong to this bounded system as one whole when the whole is considered at rest?
This is not the kinetic energy of the whole moving through an external frame.
Whole-system motion has been removed from the account.
This is not merely one internal component.
Internal kinetic energy, binding energy, stress, thermal energy, field contributions, and other carried contributions may all matter if they belong to the same bounded whole.
Structurally, rest-energy is the whole-owned energy account of the bounded system when whole-system motion is removed from the question, including internal carried contributions that belong to the same object.
If total bound rest-energy accounting fails, the system may still have energetic contributions. Internal activity may still occur. Local transfers may still be meaningful. But those contributions cannot be held as one invariant rest-frame energy burden of the bounded whole.
The system still has energies, but no one rest-energy.
That is the failure of the energy register.
Two Registers, Not Yet One Equation
At this point, the two registers must remain distinct.
The inertial register asks what burden must be answered to accelerate the bounded whole.
The energy register asks what rest-frame energy burden belongs to the bounded whole.
If these are collapsed too early, the equation becomes a slogan.
If they are separated permanently, the equation fails.
The work of E₀ = mc² is to hold these two registers as one invariant burden of the same bounded rest-frame whole.
That identity is earned by Coherence, not assumed. 
The No-Ghost Test
The inertial register and the energy register are distinct, but they cannot be allowed to drift apart inside the same bounded whole.
If something belongs to the bounded rest-frame whole as energy but does not answer in whole-system acceleration, then the system has ghost energy.
Ghost energy is energy owned by the whole that does not burden motion-change of the whole.
If something burdens whole-system acceleration but does not appear in the bounded system’s rest-energy account, then the system has ghost inertia.
Ghost inertia is inertia with no owned rest-frame energy burden.
Either failure splits the object.
In the first case, the system owns energy that acceleration does not have to answer to.
In the second case, the system carries inertial burden that the rest-energy account does not own.
Both cases produce two accounts of the same bounded whole.
That is not yet the full identity argument, but it is the first necessary constraint:
No owned energy without inertial answerability.
No inertial answerability without owned rest-energy burden.
The same bounded whole cannot carry energy that is absent from its acceleration burden, nor acceleration burden that is absent from its rest-energy account, without ceasing to have one coherent whole-object burden.
Coherence and the Whole-Owned Invariant
The no-ghost test establishes co-registration.
Co-registration alone is not enough.
Two registers could track one another without being the same burden. Two quantities can be correlated, even perfectly, while still remaining conceptually separate.
The rest-frame equation requires more than correlation.
It requires one invariant burden read in two registers.
Coherence does that work. 
A Coherence-closed bounded whole carries one whole-owned invariant under lawful repartition.
The invariant content of the same claimed whole must remain one whole-owned invariant when the system is lawfully redescribed, decomposed, recombined, or accounted through different registers.
If the bounded whole carried two independent invariants that merely happened to agree, then those invariants would remain independently decouplable.
But decoupling is exactly the failure signature.
The whole would retain one account as inertial burden and another account as rest-energy burden. The registers would remain intelligible, but the whole would no longer close as one invariant object.
Therefore, in the Coherence-closed rest-frame object, inertial answerability and total bound rest-energy accounting are not two separate invariants that happen to track.
They are two registers of one invariant burden.
The inertial register reads the burden as what must be answered to accelerate the whole.
The energy register reads the burden as what belongs to the whole as rest-energy.
The object is the same.
The burden is the same.
The registers differ.
That is the structural content licensed by Coherence.

A bounded rest-frame whole reaches the E₀ = mc² seam when its inertial answerability and its total bound rest-energy accounting are Coherence-licensed as two registers of one whole-owned invariant.

It means that, for a bounded system considered at rest, the burden that must be answered to accelerate the whole and the burden that accounts as the whole’s rest-energy are the same invariant burden read through two registers.
One bounded whole.
One whole-owned invariant.
Two registers.
Inertia and rest-energy.
